James Wan(born 26 February 1977) is an Australian filmmaker. He has primarily worked in the horror genre as the co-creator of the Saw and Insidious franchises and the creator of The Conjuring Universe. The lattermost is the highest-grossing horror franchise at over $2 billion.[2] Wan is also the founder of film and television production company Atomic Monster Productions.

James Wan is an undisputed horror legend, and he has been responsible for directing some of the most beloved blockbusters of our time. Wan’s low-budget short film Saw was picked up and turned into the massive horror franchise we all know today, spawning several successful spin-offs and continued blockbuster success. Saw was unlike any horror film which had been previously released, combining gore with shocking twists and a captivating story.
